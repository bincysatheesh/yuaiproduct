Get Started

Zambia malayalee cultural association(ZMCA).

       Established in 1980 and registered under the Societies Act of Zambia, the Zambia Malayalee Cultural Association (ZMCA) stands as a testament to the enduring commitment to cultural exchange and charitable endeavors.

        Rooted in the vibrant heritage of " God's own Country " ,Kerala, the association actively engages in diverse charitable and cultural activities to foster a strong connection with Zambia. With a core focus on preserving and upholding the rich identity and traditions of Kerala, ZMCA encourages its members to participate wholeheartedly. By providing various opportunities, the association not only nurtures cultural bonds but also serves as a platform for members to enhance their skills and talents.

Our Story

        Inaugurated in 1980 and officially recognized under the Societies Act of Zambia, the Zambia Malayalee Cultural Association (ZMCA) has been an unwavering force in fostering cultural exchange and charitable initiatives. From its inception, ZMCA dedicated itself to a dual mission: preserving the rich heritage of 'God's own Country,' Kerala, and actively engaging in charitable pursuits in Zambia.

        Embodying the spirit of cultural preservation, the association endeavors to maintain the identity and traditions that define Kerala. This commitment is reflected in various charitable and cultural activities undertaken by ZMCA. Beyond geographical borders, the association seeks to establish and nurture a profound connection with Zambia.

        Moreover, ZMCA goes beyond mere cultural preservation by providing its members with diverse opportunities. The association actively encourages participation in a myriad of activities, aiming to not only bolster cultural ties but also to enhance the skills and talents of its members.

        In essence, the Zambia Malayalee Cultural Association (ZMCA) serves as a bridge between 'God's own Country' and the heart of Africa, tirelessly working towards cultural enrichment and charitable impact in the Zambian community.
